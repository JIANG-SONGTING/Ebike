6. Discussion

6.1 Achievement of Objectives

This project set out five objectives. Four of them were fully met, and one was partially met.

The first objective, temperature monitoring, was met. The system successfully detected temperature changes and triggered alerts at all three configured thresholds of 25, 40, and 50 °C during testing.

The second objective, voltage monitoring, was partially met. The ADC measurement circuit showed an average error of 1.0% compared to a multimeter, which confirms that the circuit and firmware logic are working correctly. However, the system was only tested with the stable USB supply rail reading rather than a real battery that changes voltage during charging and discharging, so dynamic battery behaviour has not been validated.

The third objective, motion data logging with GPS, was met. The gyroscope threshold successfully triggered a Large-angle rollover alert when the MPU-6050 was rotated sharply, and the GPS coordinates at the time of the event were recorded correctly on the platform.

The fourth objective, local OLED display, was met. The temperature and voltage readings shown on the display matched the values in the mobile app, and the display updated within one second of any change in sensor readings.

The fifth objective, remote monitoring and alerting via 4G, was met. All five data streams were uploaded to the OneNET platform at once per second with no dropouts, and the alert processing speed was approximately two seconds from the moment a threshold was exceeded to the moment the alert appeared in the app.

6.2 System Performance

The DS18B20 gave stable readings across all three temperature threshold tests with no manual calibration needed. Its factory calibration removes a common source of error in analog sensor circuits, which is particularly valuable in a prototype environment where time and resources for individual sensor calibration are limited. The ADC average error of 1.0% is adequate for detecting significant voltage anomalies. For a lithium-ion cell with a nominal voltage of around 3.7V, a 1.0% error corresponds to approximately 37 mV, which is small compared to the voltage changes of tens to hundreds of millivolts that are associated with genuine fault conditions. The gyroscope threshold of 500 degrees per second reliably detected a sharp rotation and triggered the Large-angle rollover alert during testing. The 2-second alert processing time is acceptable because the system's purpose is post-event notification and data logging rather than real-time physical intervention.

The GPS field test finding is notable beyond this project. The single-constellation GT-U7 repeatedly failed to maintain a fix in Singapore's CBD, while the multi-constellation AIR780EX maintained reliable positioning in the same conditions. Dense high-rise urban environments are common in Singapore and other Asian cities where electric motorcycles are widely used. This finding suggests that multi-constellation GNSS should be the baseline requirement for any IoT system that needs reliable urban positioning in this region, not just for this project.

6.3 Limitations

Five limitations were identified in the current prototype. First, voltage monitoring was only validated with a stable USB supply rail reading, so the system's behaviour under a real battery that changes voltage over time has not been confirmed. Second, the system can only monitor and alert but cannot physically disconnect the battery when a fault is detected, which means it depends entirely on the rider or a remote person taking action after receiving an alert. Third, the breadboard prototype is not suitable for motorcycle deployment because breadboard connections can come loose under vibration and have higher contact resistance than a PCB. Fourth, the single DS18B20 sensor may miss hotspots in other parts of a multi-cell battery pack, since temperature distribution across cells is not uniform. Fifth, the system does not measure current, and overcurrent conditions are a common precursor to thermal runaway, so their absence limits the system's fault detection coverage.

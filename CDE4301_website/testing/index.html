5.2 T1: Temperature Monitoring

The DS18B20 was warmed by hand for the 25 °C threshold, and by hair dryer for the 40 and 50 °C thresholds. The OLED display updated the temperature reading in real time throughout each test. The mobile app showed an alert in all three cases.

5.3 T2: Voltage Monitoring

With the system powered by USB, the resistor divider samples the supply rail and the firmware reconstructs a reading of approximately 3.0V. The threshold was set to 3.0V, so an alert was triggered when the reading reached the threshold. ADC readings were compared against a multimeter at the same measurement point at three time points.

5.4 T3: Motion Data and Impact Detection

The MPU-6050 was rotated sharply by hand to produce a gyroscope reading that exceeded the threshold of 500 degrees per second. The mobile app displayed a Large-angle rollover message and an alert was triggered on the platform. The GPS coordinates recorded at the time of the event matched the known test location.

[Figure 6: Screenshot of app showing Large-angle rollover message and GPS coordinates — to be inserted]

5.5 T4 and T5: OLED Display and Cloud Data Logging

The OLED display showed correct temperature and voltage readings during normal operation, matching the values in the app. All five data streams, which are temperature, voltage, accelerometer, gyroscope, and GPS, updated at once per second with no dropouts over a five-minute test period.

5.6 T6: Alert Processing Speed

The OneNET platform displayed the alert approximately 2 seconds after the temperature reading exceeded the 25 °C threshold.

3. System Design

3.1 System Overview

The system has three layers. The sensing layer includes the DS18B20 temperature sensor, the MPU6050 IMU, and a resistor divider circuit connected to the STM32 ADC. The processing layer is the STM32F103C8T6 microcontroller, which reads all sensors and manages communication. The communication layer is the AIR780EX 4G and GNSS module, which uploads data to the OneNET cloud platform and retrieves GPS coordinates. A local OLED display provides real-time feedback to the rider.

3.2 System-Level Design Decisions

Before finalising the design, several system-level alternatives were evaluated. The table below summarises these decisions.

The original project scope was telematics only. After reviewing the 400V battery safety risks, the scope was expanded to include battery monitoring. A battery-monitoring-only approach was also rejected because it ignores the crash safety requirement. The integrated approach was selected since the MPU6050 and GPS were already in the existing hardware, making the expansion low-cost.

Bluetooth was evaluated for connectivity because it reduces power consumption and eliminates the SIM card requirement. It was rejected because its 10-metre range fails in crash scenarios where the phone and motorcycle can separate, and because an unconscious rider cannot relay alerts. 4G direct-to-cloud was selected because it operates independently of any paired device. The higher power consumption and SIM cost were accepted as necessary trade-offs.

For the GPS module, the Goouuu Tech GT-U7 (u-blox 6, GPS-only, single constellation) was used initially. Field testing in the Robinson Road area of Singapore's Central Business District showed repeated GPS fix loss due to tall buildings blocking the sky. The AIR780EX was adopted as a replacement because its GPS and BeiDou multi-constellation GNSS maintained reliable positioning in the same conditions. It also replaced the separate WH-LTE-7S1 4G module, reducing the system from two UART interfaces to one.

3.3 Hardware Component Selection

Each component was evaluated on cost, performance, and ease of manufacturing. Because the industry partner is a small startup company, cost-effectiveness was a primary consideration. All prices are at single-unit quantity from DigiKey or Newark (element14), April 2026.

The STM32F103C8T6 (USD 3.04, Newark) was specified by the industry partner. It supports two hardware UARTs, two I2C buses, a 12-bit ADC, and 1-Wire GPIO at 72 MHz, and is firmware-compatible with the existing prototype. The ESP32 (~USD 4.00) was rejected because its built-in WiFi is not needed at the system level. The ATmega328P-PU (USD 2.28) was rejected due to its 10-bit ADC and single UART.

The AIR780EX (~USD 15) replaced two separate modules (WH-LTE-7S1 + GT-U7, ~USD 18 combined), saving one UART interface and providing multi-constellation GNSS confirmed reliable in Singapore CBD field testing. The SIM7600G + separate GPS (~USD 22) was rejected as it costs more and still requires two modules.

The DS18B20+ (USD 4.23, DigiKey) is factory-calibrated to ±0.5°C and uses a single GPIO pin. The NTC thermistor (USD 0.48) was rejected due to calibration complexity. The LM35DZ (USD 1.73) was rejected for its limited range and ADC channel conflict.

The MPU-6050 (USD 6.88, LCSC) was specified by the partner and retained for compatibility. The LSM6DSOTR (USD 3.51) would be better for a new design. The BMI160 is obsolete at DigiKey (2024).

The ADC + Resistor Divider (under USD 0.01) showed 1.0% average error in testing. The INA219AIDR (USD 1.89) and BQ76920RGER (~USD 4.50) were rejected as over-specified for the prototype stage.

3.4 Prototype Implementation

The prototype was built on a breadboard and powered via USB. The resistor divider connects to the USB supply rail as a stable ADC input for circuit and firmware validation. Temperature monitoring, motion data logging, OLED display, 4G communication, and cloud data logging are all working. Voltage monitoring is validated at the circuit and firmware level. The full schematic is in Appendix A of the written report.
